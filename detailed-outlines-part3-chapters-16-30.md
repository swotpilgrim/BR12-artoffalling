## Chapter 16: Overheard

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words  
**POV:** Sophie Bennett, first person  
**Setting:** Dorm common room party  
**Time:** Saturday evening, mid-November

**Purpose:** Have Sophie overhear cruel conversation about herself that shatters her tentative happiness.

**Beats:**
- Rachel convinces Sophie to attend larger party in dorm common room; argues that hiding will make rumors worse if anyone has noticed Sophie's absences
- Sophie arrives to find Cameron there with crew team friends; they acknowledge each other carefully, maintaining public distance that now feels painful
- Sophie mingles with other scholarship students, trying to appear normal while hyper-aware of Cameron across room

**Dialogue Topics:** Overheard conversation about Sophie being social climber, speculation about her motives for being close to Cameron, Tyler's assessment that Cameron is being used, dismissive comments about scholarship students in general.

**Emotional Arc:** Sophie moves from nervous attendance at party to complete humiliation as she hears how Cameron's friends view her, ending with devastation and questioning whether relationship is worth this pain.

**Key Details:** Large party setting, maintaining careful distance from Cameron, going to find bathroom, hallway conversation overheard, Tyler's cruel assessment, social climbing accusations, leaving without goodbye, walking alone in darkness, humiliation and hurt.

## Chapter 17: Confrontation

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words  
**POV:** Sophie Bennett, first person  
**Setting:** The Point  
**Time:** Morning, day after party

**Purpose:** Have Sophie end relationship with Cameron because she refuses to be his secret.

**Beats:**

- Walks away refusing to look back; Cameron stands alone at The Point, realizing he's lost her by being too afraid to choose her publicly

**Dialogue Topics:** Sophie recounting overheard conversation, discussion of being kept secret, Sophie's ultimatum about going public, Cameron's explanations about complications, argument about reputation versus relationship, Sophie ending things, Cameron's protests.

**Emotional Arc:** Sophie moves from hurt and angry determination to heartbroken but resolute as she ends relationship, choosing her dignity over being someone's secret.

**Key Details:** The Point as breakup location, morning meeting, Sophie's anger and hurt, Cameron's hesitation when asked about going public, silence that damns him, walking away without looking back, Cameron left alone.

**Dialogue Topics:** Cameron's apology and explanation of what changed, discussion of his fears about judgment and expectations, Sophie's questions about his certainty, negotiation of terms for trying again, commitment to being public about relationship, plans for regatta and aftermath.
